Our apps

There are 5 applications in the Saldo Apps portfolio, and more are yet to come. Our apps enter TOP-5 App Store in their categories.

ORTY POS

All-in-one order management system with a built-in online shop. Orty helps shops in retail, HORECA, and professional services to take orders from website, delivery, counter, or tables.

Invoice Maker

Invoice generation and accounting. The app allows generating, sharing, and accounting professional invoices.

Receipt Scanner

Allows receipts scanning and creating expense reports to track your business and personal accounting. OCR technology automatically reads and codes the receipt detail.

Mileage Tracker

Mileage tracker to account for tax deduction eligibility. The app allows tracking miles and creating logs that follow IRS mileage standards.

Checkpie

Facilitates splitting the restaurant bill for groups. The app allows scanning restaurant bills, distributing dishes between the participants, and conducting mutual settlements online

Media about us

Ukrainian startup Saldo Apps wants to fit small businesses from the USA to Asia into a smartphone. What is their secret?The founders of Saldo Apps are not going to settle in any country. Invoice Maker will be promoted in the USA, ORTY – in the CIS and Southeast Asia, new products – around the world.

This year, the startup decided to scale to develop an international product for medium and small businesses. So Orty merged with Saldo Apps, which focused on working with independent contractors.
The name was left to “Saldo Apps” as more appropriate for the holding company. Today it is part of the Netpeak Group, which is owned by Artem Borodatyuk from Odessa. Companies, teams and assets were merged.

Orty is developing a single CRM system for the restaurants. It allows to track the status of orders, costs, inventory and in general everything that happens in your restaurant. The startup was founded by businessman Mykhailo Popchuk. The company managed to take third place in the IT Arena Startup Competition 2020 and receive $ 5,000 for development.

Started earlier this year, the remote-first Saldo Apps is building a data “platform” and API-driven micro-services that it says can integrate with any payment stack. The end goal is to drive better performance and “optimization” of a business’ existing payments infrastructure.
